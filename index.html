MASAI-group: Research in AI

Strategic Multi-Agent Systems for Trustworthy AI.

We are an interdisciplinary research group exploring the foundations and frontiers of intelligent, trustworthy networked systems. Our work spans complex networks and multi-agent systems, generative AI and multimedia forensics, formal methods and deepfake detection, with a shared goal of understanding, shaping, and securing large-scale interconnected digital ecosystems. By combining rigorous theory with data-driven methods, we design principled algorithms and tools to make modern AI-enabled systems more robust, explainable, and resilient in the real world.

About us

MASAI-group in a nutshell

WWe are an interdisciplinary research group studying intelligent and trustworthy networked systems thorugh neuro-symbolic AI pipelines.

Vision Trustworthy AI

We envision AI systems composed of multiple interacting agents that are not only powerful, but also explainable, reliable, and aligned with human values. We believe that formal models and logics are key to understanding and shaping the behaviour of such Complex systems especially in fields like Forensic AI.

By combining theoretical research with practical tools, we aim to close the gap between formal guarantees and real-world deployments.

Expertise Theory & practice

Our expertise spans from the design of new strategic logics for multi-agent interaction, to Complex Systems algorithms, model synthesis, and the integration of verification engines into simulation environments and industrial frameworks.

Multi-agent systems Alternating-time Temporal Logic (ATL) Natural ATL (NatATL) Model checking Game-theoretic reasoning AI in safety-critical domains
Research areas

What we work on

Our research covers foundational aspects of multi-agent systems and complex networks in real-world applications as forensic AI.

Strategic logics Foundations

We study and extend logics such as ATL and NatATL to reason about what coalitions of agents can achieve under different information and resource assumptions in multi-agent scenarios.

Expressiveness & complexity Strategy quantification Incomplete information

Multi-Agent Systems in Complex Networks Graph-based Models

We study multi-agent systems as evolving complex networks, where agent interactions, influence, and strategic behaviour emerge from structured graph topologies. Our work focuses on understanding how connectivity, network dynamics, and structural patterns shape collective decision-making and coalition formation.

Graph theory & topology Network dynamics Centrality & influence models Strategic propagation

Applications in Forensic AI Real-world Impact

We apply strategic reasoning and formal analysis to forensic AI, supporting decision-making processes in legal, investigative, and criminological contexts. Our methods aim to ensure transparency, robustness, and traceability in systems used for evidence evaluation, behavioural modelling, and risk assessment.

Explainable forensic models Risk & behaviour analysis Legal decision support Trustworthy AI pipelines

How we collaborate

We are particularly interested in interdisciplinary projects where strategic reasoning, formal guarantees, complex network dynamics, and forensic AI intersect. Our expertise spans theory and applications, enabling collaborations across scientific, industrial, and investigative domains. Examples include:

  • Formal verification and synthesis methods for multi-agent and AI-driven systems
  • Analysis and modelling of complex networks with nonlinear and emergent dynamics
  • Development of forensic AI tools for explainable, robust, and legally-informed decision support
  • Integration of multi-agent reasoning into simulations, digital twins, and analytical pipelines

If you have an idea, we are happy to discuss how MASAI-group can help.
