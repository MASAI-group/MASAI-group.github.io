MASAI-group: Research in AI

Strategic Multi-Agent Systems for Trustworthy AI.

We are an interdisciplinary research group exploring the foundations and frontiers of intelligent, trustworthy networked systems. Our work spans complex networks, generative AI, multi-agent systems, multimedia forensics, formal methods, and deepfake detection, with a shared goal of understanding, shaping, and securing large-scale interconnected digital ecosystems. By combining rigorous theory with data-driven methods, we design principled algorithms and tools to make modern AI-enabled systems more robust, explainable, and resilient in the real world.

About us

MASAI-group in a nutshell

We are an interdisciplinary research group studying intelligent and trustworthy networked systems through neuro-symbolic AI pipelines.

Vision Trustworthy AI

We envision AI systems composed of multiple interacting agents that are not only powerful, but also explainable, reliable, and aligned with human values. We believe that formal models and logics are key to understanding and shaping the behaviour of such Complex systems especially in fields like Forensic AI.

By combining theoretical research with practical tools, we aim to close the gap between formal guarantees and real-world deployments.

Our Values Core Beliefs

Our work is grounded in Rigorous Integrity, ensuring every result is reproducible and theoretically sound. We champion Open Science by sharing our datasets and tools to accelerate global progress, and we uphold Ethical Responsibility, prioritizing the societal safety of the AI systems we analyze.

Scientific Rigor Open Collaboration Ethical AI

Statement on Teamwork Our Philosophy

At MASAI-group, we operate as a flat, distributed collective where innovation outweighs hierarchy. We adopt an Agile Research methodology, utilizing weekly cross-disciplinary syncs to iteratively refine mathematical models and codebases.

We cultivate a culture of psychological safety, where constructive dissent is encouraged as the fastest path to scientific truth, ensuring that every PhD candidate contributes to shaping our strategic direction.

Expertise Theory & practice

Our expertise spans from the design of new strategic logics for multi-agent interaction, to Complex Systems algorithms, model synthesis, and the integration of verification engines into simulation environments and industrial frameworks.

Research areas

What we work on

Our research covers foundational aspects of multi-agent systems and complex networks in real-world applications as forensic AI.

Strategic logics Foundations

We study and extend logics such as ATL and NatATL to reason about what coalitions of agents can achieve under different information and resource assumptions in multi-agent scenarios.

Expressiveness & complexity Strategy quantification Incomplete information

Multi-Agent Systems in Complex Networks Graph-based Models

We study multi-agent systems as evolving complex networks, where agent interactions, influence, and strategic behaviour emerge from structured graph topologies. Our work focuses on understanding how connectivity, network dynamics, and structural patterns shape collective decision-making and coalition formation.

Graph theory & topology Network dynamics Centrality & influence models Strategic propagation

Multimedia Forensics & Security AI Robustness

Investigating the integrity of digital media in the era of Generative AI. My research focuses on exposing synthetic content and defending forensic detectors against adversarial attacks, ensuring reliability in hostile environments.

Marco Aruta

ITEE PhD Student, XL Cycle

Formal Methods applications into MAS real-world scenarios

I am a Ph.D. student (40th cycle) in Information Technology and Electrical Engineering (ITEE) at the University of Naples Federico II, and a member of the renowned Automated Strategic Reasoning (ASTREA) group under the supervision of Professor Aniello Murano. My doctoral research, titled 'New application frontiers in the development of formal methods for multi-agent systems', explores the intersection of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and formal methods, with a focus on multi-agent strategic reasoning. I work on developing AI-driven algorithms and tools for the synthesis of resilient strategies in safety-critical systems. By leveraging formal verification and knowledge representation, my research aims to enhance the reliability and robustness of autonomous decision-making processes, particularly in high-stakes domains such as medical systems.

How we collaborate

We are particularly interested in interdisciplinary projects where strategic reasoning, formal guarantees, complex network dynamics, and forensic AI intersect. Our expertise spans theory and applications, enabling collaborations across scientific, industrial, and investigative domains. Examples include:

  • Formal verification and synthesis methods for multi-agent and AI-driven systems
  • Analysis and modelling of complex networks with nonlinear and emergent dynamics
  • Development of forensic AI tools for explainable, robust, and legally-informed decision support
  • Integration of multi-agent reasoning into simulations, digital twins, and analytical pipelines

If you have an idea, we are happy to discuss how MASAI-group can help.
